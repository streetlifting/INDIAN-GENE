Streetlifting

Streetlifting is a strength sport that consists of 2 main
Formats:1.(Classic) including Weighted Pull Up and Weighted Dips. 2.(NEW4) including Weighted Pull Up,Weighted Dips,Weighted Muscel Ups and Squats. The goal is to lift the heaviest weight possible for a single repetition in each lift.

Why Streetifting?

  • Develops a muscular and powerful upper body.
  • Improves body weight mastery.
  • Combines Strength and Skill,Less Equipment, More Gains.
  • Can have multiple subdisciplines.

Powerlifting Training Basics

Streetlifting training is structured around progressive overload and includes:

  • Strength-focused workouts (3-5 reps per set).
  • Accessory exercises for weak points.
  • Periodization for long-term progress.
  • Deload weeks to prevent injuries.

Rules of Powerlifting

Streetlifting competitions are conducted under strict rules. Each lift has specific requirements:

  • Squat: The lifter must lower their hips below knee level.
  • Pull Ups: a valid pull-up starts from a dead hang (elbows locked), requires the chin to pass the bar without kipping, and ends with full arm extension.
  • Dips: a valid dip starts with locked-out arms, requires lowering until the shoulders go below the elbows, and ends with full arm extension without swinging.
  • muscle-ups:a valid muscle-up starts from a dead hang, requires a strict pull-up (chin over bar), a smooth transition over the bar, and ends with full arm extension in the dip position without kipping.

Essential Equipment

Streetlifting requires specialized equipment for safety and performance:

    FOR SQUATS

  • Powerlifting Belt: Supports the lower back and core.
  • Knee Sleeves: Provides joint support for squats.
  • Wrist Wraps: Stabilizes the wrists during heavy lifts.
  • Deadlift Shoes: Flat-soled shoes for better grip and force transfer.
  • Singlet: Mandatory attire in competitions.

    FOR DIPS,PULL-Ups,MUSCEL-Ups

  • Dip Belt:for the weights to be added during the lift
  • Elbow Sleves:for the elbow support during the dips
