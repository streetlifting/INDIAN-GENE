Powerlifting

Powerlifting is a strength sport that consists of three main lifts: the Squat, Bench Press, and Deadlift. The goal is to lift the heaviest weight possible for a single repetition in each lift.

Why Powerlifting?

  • Develops maximum strength and muscular endurance.
  • Improves bone density and joint stability.
  • Enhances athletic performance in various sports.
  • Boosts confidence and mental toughness.

Powerlifting Training Basics

Powerlifting training is structured around progressive overload and includes:

  • Strength-focused workouts (3-5 reps per set).
  • Accessory exercises for weak points.
  • Periodization for long-term progress.
  • Deload weeks to prevent injuries.

Rules of Powerlifting

Powerlifting competitions are conducted under strict rules. Each lift has specific requirements:

  • Squat: The lifter must lower their hips below knee level.
  • Bench Press: The bar must touch the chest, and the lifter must pause before pressing up.
  • Deadlift: The bar must be lifted in one motion without hitching.

Essential Equipment

Powerlifting requires specialized equipment for safety and performance:

  • Powerlifting Belt: Supports the lower back and core.
  • Knee Sleeves: Provides joint support for squats.
  • Wrist Wraps: Stabilizes the wrists during heavy lifts.
  • Deadlift Shoes: Flat-soled shoes for better grip and force transfer.
  • Singlet: Mandatory attire in competitions.
